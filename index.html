Chapter IV — How I Actually Work
The Honest Version
"I don't memorise syntax. I know where to look, how to evaluate what I find, and how to tell when I'm wrong."

When I face a problem, I think through multiple approaches first. I use documentation, research, and AI tools to inform the decision — then I pick the solution that actually fits, not just the first one that doesn't result in errors.

On AI specifically: I use it constantly, and I review everything it produces. Never trust, always verify — you keep an eye on it the same way you'd keep an eye on a toddler near something expensive.

When something breaks — and it will — I stay calm, isolate it, fix it, document what happened, and prevent it next time. The tools I use today are better than six months ago. In six months, they'll be better again. That's the job.
